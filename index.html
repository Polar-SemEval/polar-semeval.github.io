POLAR @ SemEval-2026
Task 9

Detecting Multilingual, Multicultural and Multievent Online Polarization

Polarization is the sharp division of opinions into opposing groups, often with hostility and exclusion. This shared task seeks to advance understanding of how polarization appears in text across languages, cultures, and events. Participants will develop models to detect and interpret polarized content from contexts like elections, conflicts, protests, and debates.

Key Information

21 Languages

German, Spanish, English, Arabic, Hausa, Urdu, Amharic, Italian, Russian, Myanmar, Chinese, Nepalish, Hindi, Telugu, Persian, Turkish, Bangladish, Khmer, Odia, Punjabi, Swahili.

3 Tasks

Polarization Detection, Polarization Type Classification, and Manifestation Identification across multiple languages and cultural contexts.

Important Dates

Training data ready: September 10, 2025. Evaluation period: January 10 - February 1, 2026. Workshop: Summer 2026.

Evaluation

All tasks evaluated using Macro F1 score across different languages and cultural contexts.
