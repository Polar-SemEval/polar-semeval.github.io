Subtasks

Participants may choose to compete in one or more of the three subtasks. A few sample instances are provided here for review, and additional trial data can be found at: Trial Data

Subtask 1: Polarization Detection

Binary classification to determine whether a post contains polarized content (Polarized or Not Polarized).

id text polarization
1 Find yourself a west bank settler gf 1
2 Fascist oligarchs now control the USA 1
3 Someone end this lunatic before he starts ethnic cleansing 1
4 The EU is increasing military aid to Ukraine to one billion euros the press release on the website of the Council of Europe. 0
5 House drafts bill to strike Iran proxies amid IsraelHamas 0
6 Contested races across county early voting 0
  • Go to Subtask-1 Competition

Subtask 2: Polarization Type Classification

Multi-label classification to identify the target of polarization as one of the following categories: Gender/Sexual, Political, Religious, Racial/Ethnic, or Other.

text political racial/ethnic religious gender/sexual other
Find yourself a west bank settler gf 1 0 0 1 0
Fascist oligarchs now control the USA 1 0 0 0 0

Subtask 3: Manifestation Identification

Multi-label classification to classify how polarization is expressed, with multiple possible labels including Vilification, Extreme Language, Stereotype, Invalidation, Lack of Empathy, and Dehumanization.

Languages Covered

The task covers 21 languages across different cultural and geographical contexts:

German, Spanish, English, Arabic, Hausa, Urdu, Amharic, Italian, Russian, Myanmar, Chinese, Nepalish, Hindi, Telugu, Persian, Turkish, Bangladish, Khmer, Odia, Punjabi, Swahili.
