The Task

Polarization is the division of opinions into sharply contrasting groups, often with hostility or exclusion. In the digital era it’s rising—a challenge we must address. P↔L↔R Task @ SemEval 2026 is the first initiative focused on computational approaches to detecting and interpreting polarization worldwide.

This shared task brings together researchers and practitioners to build models that detect and analyze polarization across languages, cultures, and events—advancing both technology and social understanding.

Scope:
  • Multilingual (10+ languages, incl. mid/low-resource & Global South)
  • Multicultural & event-driven: elections, conflicts, protests, social debates
  • Real online discourse: news, forums, Reddit, Bluesky, blogs

Languages

  • Annotated English, German, Spanish, Arabic, Urdu, Amharic, Hausa
  • New Turkish, Italian, Burmese, Chinese
  • Extensions Mozambican Portuguese, Kinyarwanda, Igbo, Twi, Swahili, isiXhosa, Zulu, Emakhuwa

Task Format & Subtasks

  1. Polarization Detection Subtask 1 — Binary: Is text polarized?
  2. Polarization Type Subtask 2 — Classify the target (political, religious, ethnic, gender/sexuality, or other).
  3. Manifestation Identification Subtask 3 — Classify how polarization is expressed (stereotyping, vilification, extreme language, etc; multi-label).

Data Description

  • Sources: News, Reddit, blogs, Bluesky, regional forums
  • Context: Elections, conflicts, rights debates, migration, and more
  • Size: 3,000-5,000 annotated samples per language
  • Annotation: Label Studio, Prolific, Potato, Mechanical Turk

Research Contributions

  • Advance responsible AI for low-resource languages
  • Foster explainable & inclusive NLP
  • Benchmark multilingual polarization detection
  • Promote fair, transparent computational social science
